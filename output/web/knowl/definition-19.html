Definition 4.7.1. Kinetic Energy.

The translational kinetic energy of an object is: \(K_t = 1/2mv^2\text{.}\) When you make the Particle Model AssumptionÂ 2.6.3 (that is, no rotational motion), all kinetic energy is translational, and it is often simply called kinetic energy and written \(K = 1/2mv^2\text{.}\)
