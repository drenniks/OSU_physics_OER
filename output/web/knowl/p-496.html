CHANGE ME After successful completion of this chapter, you will be able to:
  1. Define work
  2. Define kinetic energy
  3. Evaluate the relationship between work and kinetic energy
  4. Represent energy concepts using multiple representations (diagrams, graphs, words, equations)
  5. Define a system
  6. Define potential energy for a system
  7. Define mechanical energy and determine when mechanical energy is conserved in a system
  8. Analyze a system using a conservation of energy approach
  9. Draw diagrams useful for analysis of a system using an energy approach
  10. Analyze energy diagrams depicting the total energy as the sum of potential and kinetic energy
  11. Analyze systems with work done by dissipative forces such as friction
  12. Differentiate between conservative and non-conservative forces in an energy framework
  13. Define momentum
  14. Define impulse
  15. Evaluate the relationship between impulse and momentum
  16. Determine when momentum is conserved
  17. Analyze systems using a conservation of momentum approach
  18. Analyze elastic and inelastic collisions
  19. Analyze explosions using a conservation of momentum approach
  20. Draw diagrams useful for analysis of a system using momentum approach
  21. Analyze systems that independently conserve momentum and energy
