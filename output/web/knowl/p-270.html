One of the most important things to understand when drawing free-body diagrams is the difference between forces acting on a system (which appear on that systemâ€™s free-body diagram) and forces acted by a system, which would instead be represented on the free-body diagram for the other system. It is also important not to include things that are not forces, such as the velocity or acceleration of the system, which would instead be represented using a motion diagram.
