Exercise 3. Practice Calculating Work.

  1. You are pulling a box across the floor using a horizontal rope with a constant tension force of 250 N. How much work have you done after the box has slid 3 m along the floor?
  2. Suppose you were to angle your rope so that it makes an angle of \(20^o\) with the horizontal (the magnitude of the tension is unchanged). How much work have you done after the box has slid 3 m along the floor?
  3. Suppose you were to angle your rope so that it is oriented vertically (the magnitude of the tension is unchanged). How much work have you done after the box has slid 3 m along the floor?
  4. Why is the work you calculated for the three situations above not the same?
