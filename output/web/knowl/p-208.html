Add: Last, once you have the infinitesimal \(d\vec{v}\text{,}\) you need to add together every \(d\vec{v}\) over the full times of interest, which can be done with an integral: \(\int_{v_i}^{v_f}d \vec{v} = \int_{t_i}^{t_f} \vec{a}(t) dt\text{.}\) The left hand side of this equation can be easily evaluated to give \(\Delta \vec{v} = \int_{t_i}^{t_f} \vec{a}(t) dt\text{.}\)
