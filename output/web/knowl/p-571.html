A mathematical object which has a both a magnitude (a size or amount) and a direction is called a vector. It is essential to take into account how both the magnitude and direction of a vector quantity changes when you analyze motion. A quantity that only has a magnitude is called a scalar. Suppose you have a vector \(\vec{A}\) . The negative vector \(-\vec{A}\) has the same magnitude as vector \(\vec{A}\) , but points in the opposite direction. Once you understand how to visually represent and perform operations on vectors, you will move on to understand how to manipulate them algebraically.
